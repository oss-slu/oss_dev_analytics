## Development Workflow
* Branching Strategy: Each GitHub issue must have its own dedicated branch.
* Branch Naming Convention: Use the format feature/issue-number-description or bugfix/issue-number-description.
* Pull Request (PR) Submissions: All PRs for the current sprint cycle are expected to be submitted by **11:59 PM every Friday**
* Templating: All PR's and Issues must follow the provided templates unless otherwise discussed and agreed upon by the team.

## Code Review & Merging
* Merging Schedule: Contributors can expect submitted PRs to be reviewed and merged by the following Monday.
* Communication Loop: PR comments and requested changes must be responded to within **24 hours**, unless a team member has previously communicated a reason for their unavailability.
* Review Quality: Code reviews should focus on both functionality and adherence to the project's GUIDELINES.md.

## Meeting Schedule & Availability
* In-Class Meetings: The team will meet every Monday during scheduled class time.
* Absences & Deadlines: If a member cannot attend a meeting or meet a Friday PR deadline, they must notify the team via Slack as early as possible.

## Communication Standards
* Primary Channel: All technical and logistical coordination will occur in the #oss-dev-analytics Slack channel.
* Responsiveness: All members are expected to respond on Slack within **24 hours**, unless there is a scheduled break or other extenuating circumstances.
* Transparency: Team members are expected to keep the Kanban board up to date with active issues they are working on (Slack upades are also acceptable) to avoid duplication of effort.
